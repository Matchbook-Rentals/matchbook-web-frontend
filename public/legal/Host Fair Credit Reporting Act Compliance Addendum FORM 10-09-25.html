Host Fair Credit Reporting Act Compliance Addendum

This Host Fair Credit Reporting Act Compliance Addendum (“Addendum”) supplements the MatchBook Terms and Conditions (“Agreement”) between MatchBook, LLC (“MatchBook”) and the undersigned landlord or property owner (“Host”).

By checking the acknowledgment box below, Host agrees to comply with the Fair Credit Reporting Act (15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq.) (“FCRA”), applicable state “Mini-FCRAs,” and all related consumer protection, fair housing, and privacy laws when using renter screening information through the MatchBook platform.

1.      Purpose and Permissible Use.

•         Host certifies that Derived Information (as defined in Section 3), provided by MatchBook on the platform, will be used solely for the purpose of evaluating rental applications initiated by the rental applicant (“Applicant”).

•         Host shall not use Derived Information for marketing, solicitation, resale, or any other unrelated purpose.

2.      Applicant Authorization Required. Host acknowledges and agrees that only the Applicant authorizes the procurement of a Consumer Report (which includes credit reports, criminal background checks, and other renter screening data) in connection with a rental application.

3.      Derived Information Only.

•          Derived Credit Information. Host acknowledges and agrees that MatchBook will not provide the Host with the Applicant’s full Consumer Report. Instead, MatchBook will provide information generated through proprietary models that analyze the Applicant’s credit score to produce categorical assessments for rental screening purposes only (“Derived Credit Information”). By way of example, Derived Credit Information may indicate an assessment such as “Excellent,” “Good,” or “Fair.” Derived Credit Information does not disclose, replicate, or substitute for the Applicant’s full Consumer Report or raw credit score.

 

•          Derived Background Information. Host acknowledges and agrees that MatchBook will not provide the Host with the Applicant’s full background check report. Instead, MatchBook may provide a limited summary derived from third-party background information, which may include summary of criminal history, eviction history, or similar records (“Derived Background Information”). Such summaries are intended solely for rental screening purposes and may not include complete or detailed records. Derived Background Information does not disclose, replicate, or substitute for the Applicant’s full background check or underlying source data.

 

•          Derived Information. For purposes of this Policy, “Derived Information” means, collectively, Derived Credit Information and Derived Background Information. Derived Information is provided solely for use in evaluating rental applications initiated by Applicants and may not be used for any other purpose.

4.      Adverse Action Procedures.

•         If Host takes an Adverse Action (denying an application, requiring higher rent or deposits, requiring a guarantor, or other unfavorable terms) based in whole or in part on a Derived Information, Host shall provide the Applicant with an Adverse Action Notice that includes:

o   The name, address, and telephone number of the consumer reporting agency (CRA);

o   A statement that the CRA did not make the decision and cannot explain the reasons;

o   Notice of the Applicant’s right to request a free copy of the Report within 60 days;

o   Notice of the Applicant’s right to dispute inaccuracies with the CRA; and

o   A copy of the CFPB’s “Summary of Your Rights Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act,” attached as Appendix A.

o   Timing: The notice must be provided at the time of the adverse action or within three (3) business days of the decision if delivered by mail or electronically.

5.      Risk-Based Pricing Notice.

If Host uses Derived Information to offer tenancy on terms less favorable than those offered to other Applicants (e.g., higher rent or deposit based on credit risk), Host must provide a Risk-Based Pricing Notice unless an Adverse Action Notice is already provided.

6.      Information Security and Disposal.

•         Host shall implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect Derived Information.

•         Access to Derived Information must be limited to individuals with a legitimate business need.

•         Host is responsible for retaining records of Applicant authorizations and may not combine FCRA disclosure or authorization forms with unrelated notices or documents.

•         Derived Information and related information must be securely destroyed when no longer needed, including shredding paper records and securely deleting electronic files.

•         In the event of an actual or suspected data breach, Host must promptly investigate, mitigate, notify affected Applicants and regulators as required, and cooperate with MatchBook, CRAs, and law enforcement.

•         If Host ever seeks an investigative consumer report (involving interviews with neighbors, associates, or others), Host must provide additional disclosures and notices as required under the FCRA and applicable Mini-FCRAs.

7.      Record Retention.

•         Host shall retain:

o   Derived Information only as long as necessary for the rental application or tenancy;

o   Applicant authorizations and disclosures for at least five (5) years or the duration of tenancy plus seven (7) years, whichever is longer;

o   Copies of all Adverse Action or Risk-Based Pricing Notices for at least five (5) years.

o   All retained records must be stored securely and disposed of consistent with Section 6.

8.      Compliance with Related Laws.

•         Host shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local fair housing and anti-discrimination laws, including the Fair Housing Act (42 U.S.C. § 3601 et seq.).

•         Host shall comply with the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA), including providing specific reasons for denial when required.

•         Host shall comply with applicable privacy laws and landlord-tenant laws that affect the use of Derived Information.

9.      Certification and Liability.

•         Host certifies that:

o   Derived Information will be used only for permissible purposes.

o   Derived Information will not be used in violation of equal opportunity laws.

o   Applicant authorization has been obtained before any consumer credit report is accessed.

•         Host acknowledges that non-compliance may result in civil liability (including actual damages, statutory damages, punitive damages, attorney’s fees), and criminal penalties for willful violations.

•         Host acknowledges that MatchBook makes no representations or warranties regarding the accuracy, completeness, reliability, or timeliness of any Derived Information about an Applicant. Host is solely responsible for verifying any Derived Information received through MatchBook before making rental decisions.

1.      Host agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless MatchBook from any claims, damages, or liabilities arising from Host’s failure to comply with the FCRA, state consumer reporting laws, fair housing laws, this Addendum, or the Agreement.
